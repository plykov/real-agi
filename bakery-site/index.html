Our story

No shortcuts. Just flour, water, salt & patience.

We started Zuurdesem with a single jar of wild starter on a windowsill on the Korte Heuvel. Today that same starter — we call her Truus — leavens every loaf we sell. No commercial yeast, no additives, no rush. Just the quiet chemistry of time.

The 36 hours

How a loaf comes to life.

  1. 01

    Feed the starter

    Truus gets a meal of flour and water the evening before. By morning she's bubbling and alive.

  2. 02

    Mix & autolyse

    Flour and water rest together for an hour so the gluten relaxes before salt and starter join.

  3. 03

    Fold & bulk

    A series of gentle stretches over five hours builds strength without ever kneading.

  4. 04

    Cold proof

    Shaped loaves rest overnight in the chill — this is where the deep, sour flavour develops.

  5. 05

    Bake in steam

    At 5am the stone oven hits 250°C. Twenty minutes under steam, twenty to colour the crust.
