# Mentoring — life & career navigation, the corpus's canonical first vertical

> **Up:** [Verticals — the portfolio](../README.md) · **Russian counterpart:** [`Ru/3_Verticals/mentoring/README.md`](../../../Ru/3_Verticals/mentoring/README.md)

> **Part of the Real AGI portfolio.** The first and most fully theorized vertical — the navigator pointed at a person in a life or career transition. A working but thin mentor product already exists; the deep concept lives in the five essays in this folder. **Stage:** working, thin (a strong conversational mentor; pipeline, memory, and self-learning not yet built).

A person in transition does not need answers — they need someone who reads their situation *from the outside*, names what they are avoiding, and lays out a route. That is the whole product: not "ask me anything," but "here is where you actually are, and here is the move." The lead project, a working conversational mentor assistant ("Butcher"), is valued by its users for exactly this — a cold outside read of a life/career situation and a step-by-step strategy with templates, delivered from the system's side, without comfort or accommodation. The market is people in a career or life transition who want strategy and leading, not consolation.

## How it realizes the concept

It is the *positioning layer* of the core, realized before the rest of the machine — "where am I, which vector." This is the inverse of most other cards in the portfolio: they have the pipeline but not yet the leading; mentoring has the **mentor figure** working, with live users and real feedback, but not yet the pipeline beneath it. The domain — life and career transitions, with their characteristic obstacles (sunk-cost identities, false urgency, missing leverage) — is genuine domain knowledge. On the ladder it is honestly still pre-pipeline: a strong system prompt plus a conversation, with no multi-stage scenario, no feedback loop scoring its own advice, and no memory accumulating across sessions.

## What exists today

A working conversational mentor that real people return to. What is missing to make it the flagship vertical the essays describe: a scenario engine that remembers the client across sessions and learns from the outcomes of its own advice — the pipeline + feedback + self-learning that turns a mentor *conversation* into a mentor *system*.

## Where it could go

*Hypothesis:* thicken the working mentor into a scenario engine with memory and a consequence loop, and it becomes the first full Real AGI vertical — the same engine the other cards approach from the opposite direction (pipeline-first), here approached leading-first. It is the most direct empirical evidence that the first vertical is not hypothetical: a person already pays attention to a machine that leads them rather than answers them.
