# aibook — a quest-game book that teaches AI mastery and ends in a personal Mentor

> **Up:** [Verticals — the portfolio](../README.md) · **Russian counterpart:** [`Ru/3_Verticals/aibook/README.md`](../../../Ru/3_Verticals/aibook/README.md)

> **Part of the Real AGI portfolio.** A content-production effort — an unfinished manuscript ("AI Quest") plus a semi-manual, GitHub-based pipeline for writing it. It is a domain asset for the mentoring vertical, not a running agent. **Stage:** early drafts (first phase complete: structure, templates, a few sample chapters; most level folders still empty).

"AI Quest" is a quest-game-style book that walks a non-technical reader through ten levels of AI mastery — from the art of prompting up to building agents — where each level's "boss" is one of the reader's own fears ("AI won't understand me," "this is only for programmers," "automation is magic"). The final reward at the last level is a *personal Mentor*: an AI fused with the reader's goals, values, and history that "stays beside them for life." It is written for anyone with ambition and a willingness to experiment, regardless of profession. The book is authored by a "novel as code" method: a GitHub repo, a phased backlog, and an AI agent doing much of the routine (structuring, fact-checking, research) while the author keeps creative control of the prose.

## How it realizes the concept

Two pipelines coexist, neither yet an agent. The *production* pipeline (structure → intro → levels → edit → translate) makes the artifact; it is real but human-driven. The *guidance trajectory* (intro → levels → boss → quests → reward) is the same figure the corpus designs — an expert that does not answer but leads — except here it is hand-coded into static text and executed by the reader, not by the system. So this sits low on the ladder: it is content with a content-production pipeline behind it, not feedback or self-learning. Its real value to Real AGI is conceptual and as a domain knowledge base: the book *articulates in prose* exactly the Mentor figure that the navigator core would *execute* in mechanism, and could serve as funnel/onboarding into the [mentoring](../mentoring/README.md) vertical.

## Distinct from AGIbook

[AGIbook](../agibook/README.md) is a philosophical essay-manifesto on intelligence and ontology, written with a multi-subagent (writer/editor/consistency-checker) pipeline. aibook is a practical, gamified AI-literacy guide for a mass audience. Different genre, different reader, different stage — related only in that both are book projects feeding the corpus.

## Where it could go

*Speculation:* the quest could be lifted off the page and run as an interactive guidance pipeline atop the Real AGI core — levels become stages, bosses become modeled domain obstacles, and the "personal Mentor" reward stops being a promise in text and becomes the live navigator tracking the reader's progress and adapting to them. That move (plus an explicit business model, currently absent) is the natural path from drafts toward a working vertical.
