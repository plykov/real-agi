# Strategy — a personal strategic knowledge base (the corpus's own "where to bet" archive)

> **Up:** [Verticals — the portfolio](../README.md) · **Russian counterpart:** [`Ru/3_Verticals/strategy/README.md`](../../../Ru/3_Verticals/strategy/README.md)

> **Part of the Real AGI portfolio.** This is the project closest to the navigator's *positioning layer* — not by mechanism but by subject: its entire content is the work of deciding where to bet, how to position, and which market to enter. **Stage:** working knowledge base (no code; a few hundred Markdown files), not a product on a market.

Strategy is a structured, index-navigated archive of the author's strategic thinking, converted from Notion and organized into a master index → section indexes → individual files. It collects business directions, product analytics, audience philosophy, a US market-entry plan, and a portfolio analysis of roughly a dozen sibling projects. It self-describes as "not code — a knowledge base for making decisions." The job it does is concrete: hold the reasoning behind positioning choices in one navigable place so a human (or an agent reading on the human's behalf) can decide what to build and where to compete.

## How it realizes the concept

It supplies the *content* of the positioning layer without being the *machine* that runs it. The core's loop is reality model → scenario fan → positioning, learning from labeled consequences; Strategy is a rich, hand-built reality model and a record of past positioning bets, but it does not generate a scenario fan, does not act, and does not learn from outcomes. On the ladder it is a step below a pipeline: a static, well-indexed corpus you read, not a process that moves toward a goal. Its domain (transformational AI/info business) and its market analysis are described as objects of study, not embedded in a running agent. Value to Real AGI is as *raw material* — formulated goals, obstacles, segments, and a clean articulation of the "expert that leads, not answers" pattern — rather than as engine or vertical.

## What exists today

A filled, actively maintained archive: master index, a priorities document with architecture, several section indexes, and per-project summaries of the surrounding portfolio. Zero executable files. Navigation is the only "mechanism."

## Where it could go

*Hypothesis:* the same content could become the domain knowledge base behind a positioning vertical — feeding a navigator that turns the archive's static bets into a live scenario fan with feedback. That step requires the engine, which lives elsewhere; here it remains source, not product.
